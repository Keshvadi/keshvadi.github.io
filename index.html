Sina Keshvadi

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Software Engineeting at Thompson Rivers University in Kamploos, BC, Canada.
skeshvadi@tru.ca | OM 2736 | (250) 852-7233

My research focuses on Computer Networks and Internet Measurement.
In the Inetrnet Measurement, we collect, analyze, and share data to better understand how the internet works, how it's used, and how it evolves. This work helps to inform scientific research, technology development, and public policy decisions related to the Internet.

I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from University of Calgary (2017 - 2021).
Previously, I was a teaching faculty member at the Payame Noor University, Tehran, Iran (2012 - 2017).

Research

Currently, I am investigating integrated path selection and congestion control mechanisms to enhance the performance and stability of Multipath QUIC within emerging path-aware network architectures like SCION.
Additionaly, I collaborate with colleagues in teaching and education on Scholarship of Teaching and Learning (SoTL) initiatives to contribute to the development of effective pedagogical approaches for cybersecurity education.
