Code of Conduct

To create a productive study environment, we encourage members to turn on their cameras to foster a sense of community and accountability.
Please review these guidelines to help maintain a positive and productive online study space:

  • All microphones will be muted by default during quiet study sessions.
  • Avoid visual disruptions such as flashing lights or excessive background movement.
  • Minimize rejoining the session to reduce distractions for others. If you need to take a break, please turn off your camera.
  • Respect all participants. Disruptive behavior may result in removal from the session.

Need Help During a Session?

If you encounter any issues during a StudyHub session, follow these steps:

  • Inappropriate Behavior: If someone in your group is being inappropriate or disruptive, contact the organizer privately. They will address the situation immediately.
  • Technical Issues: For help with breakout rooms, camera, or microphone settings, you can reach out to the organizer for assistance. They will do their best to trouble shoot general issues.
  • How to Contact the Session Organizer:
    1. In Microsoft Teams, click on the Chat tab.
    2. Search for the organizer’s name (visible in the participant list).
    3. Send a private message explaining the issue.

Session organizers are here to ensure a safe, respectful, and productive study environment for all participants. Please do not hesitate to reach out.

Volunteer Information

Volunteers are an essential part of the StudyHub, helping to create a collaborative and productive study environment. As a session organizer, you will guide participants, manage breakout groups, and ensure sessions run smoothly.

Why Volunteer?

  • Develop leadership and organizational skills.
  • Help fellow students reduce exam stress and improve their study habits.
  • Stay accountable and by using these scheduled sessions to focus on your own studies!

What Does a Volunteer Do?

  • Join the session 10 minutes early to prepare.
  • Admit participants from the lobby and assign them to breakout groups.
  • Ensure a respectful and focused environment during the session.
  • Provide guidance and troubleshoot any issues reported by participants.

Requirements

  • Be open to learning Microsoft Teams. Don’t worry if you’re new—we’ll provide a simple guide to help you get started with confidence.
  • Dedicate at least two 3-hour shifts. Note: These shifts will be scheduled based on your preferences and availability.
  • Have a stable Wi-Fi connection to ensure seamless participation during sessions.
  • Have access to a device with a working microphone and webcam (laptops or desktop computers are preferred).
  • Be willing to follow the StudyHub guidelines and encourage a supportive environment.

Organizer Guide

As a session co-organizer, your role is to guide participants, create breakout groups, and maintain a focused, respectful study environment. While you can study alongside participants, please keep an eye on private messages in case a participant needs help. Follow these steps to ensure a smooth session:

Before the Session

  • Join the Meeting Link: Use the StudyHub Meeting Link to enter the session 10 minutes early.
  • Check Your Role: Ensure you are listed as a "Co-Organizer" in the participants list. This allows you to manage the session.
  • Turn on Notifications: Enable notifications in Microsoft Teams to ensure you don’t miss private messages from participants:
    1. Click on the three dots ("Settings and more") next to your profile picture in the top-right corner.
    2. Select "Settings" from the dropdown menu.
    3. Go to the "Notifications" tab.
    4. Under "Chat message notifications", select "Show in banner" to receive visual alerts for private messages.
    5. Under "Sound", toggle "Play sounds for notifications" to "On", and ensure your device sound is not muted to also hear alerts for private messages.

Starting the Session

  • Admit Participants: To let participants in from the lobby, click the notification that appears when someone is waiting, or:
    1. Click on "Participants" in the top toolbar.
    2. Under the "Lobby" section, click "Admit" next to each participant's name.
  • Introduce Yourself: Begin the session by welcoming participants and explaining the session guidelines:
    • Keep microphones muted in breakout rooms.
    • Turn on your camera for accountability (optional).
    • Report any issues privately to the organizer.
  • Create Breakout Rooms: Use the Microsoft Teams "Breakout Rooms" feature to assign participants to groups of 3-4. Click the "Breakout Rooms" button and:
    1. Select the number of rooms.
    2. Choose "Assign Manually" or "Automatically".
    3. Click "Open" to send participants to their rooms.

During the Session

  • Monitor Private Messages: Watch for notifications of private messages from participants in case they need assistance.
  • Handle Issues: If a participant reports an issue (e.g., inappropriate or disruptive behaviour), address it promptly by:
    • Muting disruptive participants.
    • Reassigning groups if needed.
    • Removing disruptive members if behavior persists.

Ending the Session

  • Close Breakout Rooms: Click "Close All Rooms" to bring participants back to the main room.
  • Facilitate Group Sharing: Ask participants to briefly share what they accomplished during the session (optional).
  • Thank Participants: Conclude by thanking everyone for joining and reminding them about the next session.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

  • Can't Admit Participants: Refresh the meeting page and check your role as "Co-Organizer."
  • Participants Can't Hear Me: Check your microphone settings or switch devices in Teams under Settings > Devices.
